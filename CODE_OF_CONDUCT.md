# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We as members, contributors, and leaders commit to making participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We commit to acting and interacting in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

### ‚úÖ Positive Behaviors

- **Demonstrating empathy and kindness** toward other people
- **Being respectful** of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- **Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback**
- **Taking responsibility** and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from the experience
- **Focusing on what is best** not just for us as individuals, but for the overall community
- **Using welcoming and inclusive language**
- **Assuming good intent** in others' actions
- **Listening actively** to understand perspectives different from your own
- **Valuing diverse contributions** - code, documentation, design, testing, etc.
- **Being patient and supportive** with newcomers
- **Recognizing and celebrating** others' contributions and achievements
- **Being professional and respectful** even in disagreement
- **Keeping discussions on topic** and constructive
- **Asking questions** rather than making assumptions

### ‚ùå Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **Harassment or bullying** - including comments, jokes, or actions that demean others
- **Discrimination** based on any protected characteristic
- **Sexual or romantic harassment** - unwanted sexual comments or advances
- **Doxing or sharing private information** without consent
- **Threats of violence** or violent language directed at another person
- **Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments**
- **Personal attacks** or ad hominem arguments
- **Sustained disruption** of community discussions
- **Publishing others' private information** (doxxing)
- **Abuse of power or authority** within the community
- **Spamming** or repeatedly sending unsolicited messages
- **Plagiarism** or taking credit for others' work
- **Bad faith** arguments or intentionally misleading statements
- **Gatekeeping** or making others feel unwelcome
- **Passive-aggressive behavior**
- **Exclusionary jokes** or comments
- **Using slurs** or dehumanizing language

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

**To report a Code of Conduct violation:**

1. **Document the incident** - Note the date, time, and details of what occurred
2. **Identify participants** - Who was involved (usernames/names)
3. **Report to maintainers** - Email project maintainers with:
   - Description of the incident
   - Links to relevant messages/issues (if applicable)
   - Impact on you or the community
   - Desired resolution (if any)
   - Whether you wish to remain anonymous

**Report Template:**

```
Subject: Code of Conduct Violation Report

Reported By: [Your name or "Anonymous"]
Date: [When did this occur?]
Location: [GitHub issue, PR, discussion, email, etc.]

Incident Description:
[Describe what happened in detail]

Participants:
[Who was involved?]

Links/Evidence:
[Links to relevant comments, issues, etc.]

Impact:
[How did this affect you or the community?]

Desired Resolution:
[What would you like to happen?]
```

### Confidentiality

All reports will be handled confidentially. We will not disclose the reporter's identity without their permission, except as required by law.

### Investigation Process

When we receive a report of Code of Conduct violation:

1. **Initial Review** (24-48 hours)
   - Acknowledge receipt of report
   - Conduct preliminary assessment
   - Gather additional information if needed

2. **Investigation** (3-7 days)
   - Interview relevant parties
   - Collect evidence and documentation
   - Review context and history
   - Assess severity and impact

3. **Resolution** (varies)
   - Determine appropriate action
   - Communicate decision to reporter
   - Implement corrective measures
   - Monitor for compliance

4. **Follow-up**
   - Check in with affected parties
   - Ensure no retaliation occurs
   - Assess effectiveness of resolution

## Enforcement Actions

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action deemed in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence:** 
- A private written warning from community leaders
- Clarification of expectations and norms
- Public apology may be requested

---

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact:** A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence:**
- A warning with consequences for continued behavior
- No interaction with involved parties for specified period
- May include temporary restrictions on participation

---

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact:** A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence:**
- Temporary suspension from community interaction (7-30 days)
- No interaction with involved parties
- Violations in banned areas result in permanent ban
- Reviewer may lift ban early if evidence of rehabilitation shown

---

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence:**
- Permanent removal from community
- All content removed or anonymized
- Permanent ban from future participation
- May result in legal action if warranted

---

## Appeals Process

If you believe enforcement action was unjust or excessive:

1. **Request Appeal** - Email maintainers within 14 days with:
   - Why you believe the action was unjust
   - New information that should be considered
   - What you've done to address the violation

2. **Appeal Review** - A different person (not involved in original decision) will:
   - Review all evidence
   - Consider your appeal
   - Make final determination

3. **Appeal Decision** - You will be notified of the outcome within 7 days

**Note:** Appeals are not guaranteed and decisions may stand as-is.

## Resources and Support

### For Those Experiencing Harassment

- **Document everything** - Save messages, take screenshots, record dates/times
- **Reach out to someone** - Talk to a friend, family member, or counselor
- **Report to maintainers** - Use the reporting process above
- **Consider external resources:**
  - If experiencing serious harassment: contact local law enforcement
  - Mental health support: seek professional counseling
  - Online safety: consult cybersecurity resources

### For Those Who Caused Harm

We believe in redemption and growth. If you recognize you've caused harm:

1. **Take responsibility** - Acknowledge what you did wrong
2. **Understand impact** - Listen to how others were affected
3. **Apologize sincerely** - A genuine, non-defensive apology
4. **Make amends** - Take concrete steps to repair harm
5. **Change behavior** - Demonstrate changed behavior over time

Resources to help:
- Reflect on your actions and impact
- Seek feedback from trusted community members
- Consider perspective-taking exercises
- Read about implicit bias and inclusive communication
- Participate in conflict resolution training (if available)

## Specific Community Guidelines

### Code Review Guidelines

When reviewing code:

‚úÖ **Do:**
- Be constructive and helpful
- Explain the reasoning behind suggestions
- Acknowledge good work
- Ask questions to understand intent
- Suggest improvements, not commands
- Be specific about issues
- Provide resources or examples

‚ùå **Don't:**
- Use dismissive language
- Attack the author personally
- Reject code without explanation
- Block progress without helping fix issues
- Make demands without context
- Ignore the author's perspective
- Use sarcasm or passive-aggressive comments

**Example of Good Review Comment:**
```
Good catch on using a loop here. I'm wondering if we could
simplify this using `grep` instead? Something like:

```bash
grep "pattern" file.txt
```

This would be more efficient for large files. What do you think?
```

**Example of Bad Review Comment:**
```
This is inefficient. Use grep.
```

### Discussion Guidelines

When discussing in issues, pull requests, or discussions:

‚úÖ **Do:**
- Stay on topic
- Be respectful of different opinions
- Provide context and links
- Use threads to keep discussions organized
- Summarize conclusions
- Acknowledge when someone makes a good point
- Use `@mentions` sparingly and appropriately

‚ùå **Don't:**
- Derail discussions with off-topic comments
- Use discussions to promote unrelated projects
- Spam or send repetitive messages
- Engage in heated arguments
- Use the discussion to vent frustrations
- Make it personal
- Ignore responses to your questions

### Inclusive Language Guidelines

Use language that is welcoming to everyone:

‚úÖ **Inclusive:**
- "Hi folks" or "Hey everyone"
- "Please ping me if you have questions"
- "Maintainers"
- "Developers" or "Contributors"
- "Person with a disability"
- "Allowlist" / "Denylist"

‚ùå **Exclude or Offend:**
- "Guys" (can exclude non-male people)
- "Pinging" (may exclude those unfamiliar with term)
- "Master/Slave"
- "Rockstar" / "Ninja" developers (gatekeeping)
- "Handicapped" or "Suffers from" disability
- "Whitelist" / "Blacklist"
